Heading: Tokyo shares open higher on weaker yen stimulus 

Article: strong>TOKYO: Tokyo shares rallied at the start of trading Tuesday, following a solid lead from Wall Street and with exporters boosted by a sharply weaker yen, which eased on stimulus hopes.</strongThe benchmark Nikkei 225 index soared 2.29 percent, or 359.82 points, to 16,068.64 shortly after the opening bell, while the broader Topix index of all first-section shares jumped 2.24 percent, or 28.10 points, to 1,283.89.
