Heading: Pakistan 400 all out take 103 run lead against England

Article: Birmingham, United Kingdom, Aug 5, 2016 (AFP) -Pakistan were bowled out for 400 in reply to England´s first innings 297, a lead of 103 runs, at tea on the third day of the third Test at Edgbaston on Friday.Azhar Ali top-scored with 139 and recalled opener Sami Aslam made a Test-best 82 in an innings where Pakistan captain Misbah-ul-Haq´s 56 was his third fifty of the series.Sarfraz Ahmed was 46 not out.Chris Woakes, in the all-rounder´s first Test match on his Warwickshire home ground, led England´s attack with three for 79, while Stuart Broad took three for 83 as Pakistan kept England in the field for more than 10 hours in total.James Anderson, England´s all-time leading wicket-taker, took two for 54 in 29.1 overs before he was barred from bowling in the remainder of the innings after receiving a third warning from the umpires for running on the pitch.The four-match series is level at 1-1.
